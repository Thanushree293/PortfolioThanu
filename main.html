Mental Health website

During the CodeFury 6.0 Hackathon, our team developed a responsive mental health website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. The site was designed to address issues like information overload and social media pressure, featuring interactive elements such as self-assessment tools, a chatbot for instant support, mood trackers, a resource library, guided yoga sessions for stress relief, and a call conference feature for live support. Blind Person To Access Atm

During the Smart India Hackathon, our team created an accessible ATM interface designed for visually impaired users. Using Python, we incorporated features such as one-time password generation and facial recognition to ensure secure and seamless authentication. The interface was designed to be user-friendly, allowing visually impaired individuals to navigate and perform banking transactions independently. Our solution aimed to enhance both accessibility and security, providing a reliable and efficient banking experience tailored to the needs of users with visual impairments.

Eyeglass Erase GAN

A glasses removal system has broad applications across various industries, ranging from improving security and authentication systems to enhancing digital image editing and virtual experiences . .This project focuses on the development and implementation of a GAN model capable of generating facial images that accurately depict individuals without glasses, even if they were originally wearing eyeglasses. The proposed solution involves leveraging the adversarial learning framework of GANs to train a generator network to generate glasses-free facial images while simultaneously training a discriminator network to distinguish between real and generated images.
